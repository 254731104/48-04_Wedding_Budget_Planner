Destination Wedding Cost Calculator: Compare Top Wedding Destinations in 2026

June 1, 2025 10 min read

Destination weddings blend honeymoons with celebrations -- but they come with a unique set of costs that domestic weddings don't. Guest flights, accommodation blocks, transportation between venues, and multi-day events all factor into a destination wedding's total price tag.

In this comprehensive cost calculator, we break down the real expenses of destination weddings across four top regions: Mexico, the Caribbean, Europe, and Hawaii. Here's what each costs for a 50-guest celebration.

All-Inclusive Resort Weddings (Mexico & Caribbean)

All-inclusive resorts in Mexico and the Caribbean have revolutionized destination weddings by bundling venue, catering, accommodation, and basic decor into a single per-guest price. This makes budgeting incredibly straightforward.

For the couple: all-inclusive wedding packages often cost $8,000-$20,000 (venue, basic flowers, photography, cake, coordinator, and sometimes honeymoon suite). The guests' travel and lodging is usually covered by the resort package.

Key advantage: Predictable pricing. No surprise fees, no per-guest variances that blow up your budget.

European Destination Weddings

Europe offers unmatched romance but requires more logistical coordination and generally costs more. Couples typically fly 15-30 guests (vs. 50-100 for Mexico) because international travel from the US is pricier and longer.

European costs run higher because: (1) You're often renting private villas/chateaux + hiring separate vendors, (2) Travel costs for photographers/videographers from the US, (3) Legal paperwork for international marriages can be complex.

Hawaii Wedding Costs

Hawaii occupies a unique middle ground -- domestic (no passport needed) but priced like a destination. Oahu is the most affordable; Maui and Kauai command premium pricing.

  • Oahu (Waikiki/Kualoa Ranch): $18,000-$35,000 for 50-80 guests
  • Maui (Kapalua/Maui Beach): $25,000-$50,000 for 40-70 guests
  • Kauai (Poipu/Lihue): $22,000-$45,000 for 30-60 guests
  • Big Island (Kona): $15,000-$30,000 for 40-70 guests

Hawaii advantage: No international travel for US guests. Disadvantage: Hawaii's remote location makes everything imported, so food and supply costs are higher than mainland averages.

Hidden Destination Wedding Costs

  • Pre-wedding site visit: Flight + 3-night hotel = $1,000-$3,000 (highly recommended)
  • Second shooter travel: If bringing your photographer from home = $800-$2,000
  • Guest shuttle/transport: Airport transfers + venue shuttles = $2,000-$5,000
  • Welcome party: Destination weddings often include a Friday welcome dinner ($30-$80/guest)
  • Post-wedding brunch: Saturday morning farewell brunch = $15-$40/guest
  • Wedding coordinator on-site: Many destinations require a local planner ($2,000-$5,000)
  • Marriage license & blood work: International marriages sometimes require apostilles ($50-$200)

Is a Destination Wedding Worth It?

Worth it if: You want an intimate, meaningful celebration; your closest friends and family will attend; you value the travel experience; your budget is $20,000-$50,000 and you'd want that for a big domestic wedding anyway.

Skip it if: Only 20% of invited guests can afford to attend (creates awkward dynamics); your total budget is under $15,000 (you'll spend almost all of it on logistics); you're stressed about coordinating across time zones.

Money-Saving Tip

Book your destination wedding during shoulder season (late April-May or late August-October) for 20-30% lower resort rates. Avoid December holidays and spring break weeks entirely.
