Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about wedding budget planning

The average wedding cost varies widely depending on location and size. A small intimate wedding (under 50 guests) typically costs between $10,000-$20,000, while a medium wedding (50-100 guests) ranges from $20,000-$40,000. Large celebrations with 100+ guests can cost $40,000-$80,000+. Major cities like NYC and LA tend to be 30-50% more expensive than smaller markets.

Venue and catering combined typically account for 50-60% of the total wedding budget. The venue rental alone usually takes 25-30%, while food and beverage costs add another 20-30%. This is why choosing the right venue and managing guest count are the two most impactful ways to control your overall wedding costs.

Ideally, you should start planning your wedding budget 12-18 months before the wedding date. This gives you enough time to research costs, book vendors, and save strategically. However, even if you have less time, our budget calculator can help you quickly estimate costs and create a realistic financial plan.

Top money-saving strategies include: (1) Choose an off-peak month like January, February, or November for lower rates. (2) Reduce your guest list to the most important people. (3) Consider a non-traditional venue like a public park or community center. (4) DIY certain elements like invitations or decor. (5) Book vendors during their off-season. Our calculator helps you see exactly where your money goes so you can make informed trade-offs.
